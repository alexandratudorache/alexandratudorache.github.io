1. Rebuilding Responsive VR Shooting Loops

To make combat faster and deeper, we overalled the mechanics behind weapon responsiveness[cite: 13, 27]. We explicitly prioritized real-time visual tracking over pure aesthetic detail to ensure intense combat didn't result in processing unnecessary background noise[cite: 31, 56, 59].

  • Iterated Feedback: Tuned projectile visibility, hit audio cues, and Quest-optimized haptic loops[cite: 28, 109].
  • Readability Constraint: Simplifying overdraw effects directly decreased player frustration[cite: 59, 60].

2. Scheduling Two-Hour Progression Curves

We strictly controlled content distribution across a targeted two-hour window to keep early engagement metrics high[cite: 16, 19]:

  • First Hour: Heavily front-loaded mechanical unlocks to hook players instantly[cite: 19].
  • Second Hour: Tapered item drops to rigorously design endgame maps around a predictable player loadout[cite: 20].
